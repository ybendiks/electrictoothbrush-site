Travel Electric Toothbrush Buying Guide

TSA Rules for Electric Toothbrushes

Electric toothbrushes are permitted in both carry-on and checked bags. There are no TSA restrictions on rechargeable lithium-ion battery toothbrushes. However, if your brush has a lithium battery, it is recommended to carry it in your carry-on rather than checked luggage per general lithium battery guidelines.

Battery Life Is Everything

The most important feature for a travel electric toothbrush is battery life. A brush that dies mid-trip defeats the purpose of packing it. Look for models with at least a 14-day battery life for most trips, and 30 days or more for extended travel. Battery-powered options like Quip eliminate charging concerns entirely.

Size and Weight

Every ounce matters when packing light. Dedicated travel toothbrushes like the Philips One weigh under 4 ounces and have slim profiles that fit in any bag. Full-size brushes with travel cases work well for checked luggage but can feel bulky in carry-on toiletry bags.

Charging Flexibility

USB-C charging is the gold standard for travel because you can share cables with your phone, laptop, and other devices. Proprietary charging docks are the least travel-friendly option. Some brushes, like the Oral-B iO Series 4, include travel cases that double as chargers, which is an elegant solution.

Travel Case Quality

A good travel case protects the brush head from getting dirty or damaged in your bag, keeps the brush from accidentally turning on, and ideally ventilates to prevent mildew. Magnetic snap cases like the Philips One's are the most convenient, while hard-shell cases provide the best protection.

Our Verdict

The Philips One by Sonicare is the best travel electric toothbrush for most people. Its 30-day battery, USB-C charging, magnetic case, and ultra-slim profile make it the perfect travel companion. For those who want the absolute lightest option, Quip is hard to beat at 1.3 ounces with 3-month battery life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you bring an electric toothbrush on a plane? +

Yes, electric toothbrushes are allowed in both carry-on and checked luggage. The TSA has no restrictions on battery-powered or rechargeable electric toothbrushes. For lithium-ion battery models, carry-on is recommended per standard lithium battery guidelines.

What is the best electric toothbrush for travel? +

The Philips One by Sonicare is our top travel pick. It is compact, has a 30-day battery, includes a travel case, and uses USB-C charging available anywhere. For ultra-light travel, the Quip at 1.3 ounces with a 3-month AAA battery is the lightest option.

How long do travel electric toothbrush batteries last? +

Most travel electric toothbrushes last 2-4 weeks on a single charge. The Philips One and Burst Original both offer 30-day battery life. Battery-powered options like Quip last about 3 months on a single AAA battery, making them ideal for extended trips.
