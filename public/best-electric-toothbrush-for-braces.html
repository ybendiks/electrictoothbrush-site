Buying Guide: Electric Toothbrushes and Braces

Why Electric Toothbrushes Matter More with Braces

Braces create numerous small spaces where plaque and food particles accumulate. Studies show that electric toothbrush users with braces have 11% less plaque and significantly fewer white spot lesions (early cavities) compared to those using manual brushes.

Choosing Between Sonic and Oscillating

Both types work well with braces, but each has advantages. Sonic toothbrushes clean through fluid dynamics, driving cleaning solution between teeth and around brackets without direct contact. This reduces the risk of catching on wires. Oscillating brushes like Oral-B use a round head that can target individual brackets and the areas around them with precision.

The Importance of Pressure Sensors

Brushing too hard with braces can debond brackets, bend wires, and irritate already-sensitive gums. A pressure sensor that alerts you or automatically reduces power when you apply too much force is not just a luxury with braces; it is essential for preventing costly orthodontic repairs.

Brush Head Considerations

Replace brush heads every 6-8 weeks with braces instead of the standard 3 months. The brackets and wires cause faster bristle wear. Look for soft or extra-soft bristle options, and consider orthodontic-specific brush heads like Oral-B's Ortho head, which has a V-shaped trim to clean around brackets.

Complementary Tools

An electric toothbrush alone is not enough with braces. Pair it with a water flosser for between-bracket cleaning, interdental brushes for areas the toothbrush cannot reach, and fluoride rinse to strengthen enamel around brackets. The Waterpik Sonic-Fusion combines two of these tools into one device.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use an electric toothbrush with braces? +

Yes, electric toothbrushes are safe and actually recommended for braces. They are more effective at removing plaque around brackets and wires than manual brushing. Choose a brush with soft bristles, a sensitive mode, and ideally a pressure sensor to prevent damage to your orthodontic hardware.

What type of electric toothbrush is best for braces? +

Sonic toothbrushes are generally preferred for braces because their vibrating action cleans around brackets without catching on wires. However, oscillating brushes like Oral-B also work well, especially with their orthodontic-specific brush heads. The key features to look for are a pressure sensor, soft bristles, and a sensitive cleaning mode.

How often should you replace brush heads with braces? +

With braces, brush heads wear out faster due to contact with brackets and wires. Replace them every 6-8 weeks instead of the standard 3 months. Watch for frayed or splayed bristles as signs that a replacement is needed sooner.

Do orthodontists recommend electric toothbrushes? +

Yes, most orthodontists recommend electric toothbrushes for patients with braces. Studies show they remove significantly more plaque around orthodontic hardware than manual brushing, reducing the risk of white spot lesions and gum disease during treatment.
