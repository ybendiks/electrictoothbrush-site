Buying Guide: What Matters Most

Cleaning Action: Oscillating vs. Sonic

At this price point, you have a real choice between Oral-B's oscillating-rotating technology and Sonicare's sonic vibration. Both are clinically proven effective. Oscillating brushes use a small round head that rotates and pulsates. Sonic brushes use an elongated head that vibrates at high frequency. Try both if you can; it comes down to personal preference.

Pressure Sensor

A pressure sensor is arguably the most valuable feature you gain at this price range. Brushing too hard is the most common brushing mistake and can damage enamel and gums over time. Both the Oral-B Pro 1000 and Sonicare 4100 include this feature, which is rare under $30.

Battery Type

At this price, Oral-B models still use NiMH batteries (7-day life), while Sonicare uses lithium-ion (14-day life). If long battery life matters to you, especially for travel, the Sonicare has a clear advantage here.

Brush Head Ecosystem

Consider the long-term cost of replacement heads. Oral-B standard heads ($5-8 each) have the most variety and the most third-party options. Sonicare heads ($6-9 each) are slightly pricier but also well-supported by third parties. Burst uses proprietary heads available mainly through their subscription.

Our Recommendation

The Oral-B Pro 1000 and Sonicare 4100 are both exceptional values at $50. If you prefer the thorough scrubbing sensation of oscillating-rotating action and want maximum brush head variety, go with the Oral-B. If you prefer the gentler, buzzing feel of sonic cleaning and want longer battery life, choose the Sonicare. Either way, you are getting 90% of the cleaning performance of models costing four times as much.
