Colgate Electric Toothbrush: Complete Lineup Review and Guide

Quick Answer: Colgate offers electric toothbrushes ranging from $7 battery-powered models to the $70 Hum smart toothbrush. While Colgate is better known for toothpaste, their Hum brush delivers solid mid-range smart features at a competitive price. For budget buyers, the Colgate 360 battery series provides a basic power-assisted option at grocery store prices.

Colgate's Electric Toothbrush Lineup

Most people associate Colgate with toothpaste, and for good reason: the company has been a global leader in oral care products for over two centuries. But Colgate has been steadily expanding its electric toothbrush offerings, recognizing that the powered brushing market continues to grow as more consumers move away from manual brushes.

Colgate's current electric toothbrush lineup can be divided into three tiers: the premium Hum smart toothbrush, the mid-range ProClinical series (available in select markets), and the budget-friendly 360 battery-powered models. Each tier serves a different customer need, and understanding the differences helps you pick the right Colgate brush, if any, for your situation.

Should You Choose Colgate or a Dedicated Electric Toothbrush Brand?

Colgate's strength in the electric toothbrush market lies in accessibility and trust. The brand is available everywhere, most people already use Colgate products, and the price points are approachable. If you're shopping at a drugstore and want to grab a powered brush without much research, Colgate is a safe choice.

However, if you're willing to do a bit of comparison shopping, dedicated electric toothbrush brands like Oral-B and Sonicare offer more cleaning power per dollar. Their decades of focus on electric brushing technology translates into more refined motors, better brush head designs, and broader ecosystems of accessories and replacement parts.

The one area where Colgate genuinely competes is with the Hum's smart features. At $70, it offers app-guided brushing at a price point where most Oral-B and Sonicare options lack Bluetooth connectivity entirely. If smart tracking matters to you and you don't want to spend $200 or more on a premium smart brush, the Hum deserves consideration.

Worth Noting: Colgate has been investing heavily in oral care technology, including acquiring companies like Hello Products and PCA Skin. Their commitment to the electric toothbrush space appears to be growing, so expect more competitive offerings in the coming years.

Our Verdict

Colgate offers competent electric toothbrushes but doesn't lead in any single category. The Hum provides affordable smart features, and the 360 battery brushes are fine for an inexpensive upgrade from manual brushing. For most shoppers prioritizing cleaning performance, we'd still recommend Oral-B or Sonicare as primary choices, with Colgate as a solid backup option if those brands aren't available or don't fit your budget.
