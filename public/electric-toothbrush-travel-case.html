Best Electric Toothbrush Travel Cases (2026)

Updated: February 2026 9 min read

Whether you travel weekly for business or take a few vacations per year, a proper case is a small investment that preserves your much larger investment in the toothbrush itself.

We have tested travel cases from major brands and third-party manufacturers across dozens of trips. Here are the best options organized by brand compatibility and use case.

What to Look for in a Travel Case

Hard shell vs soft pouch. Hard shell cases provide the best protection against impact and crushing in luggage. EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate) cases offer a good balance of protection and lightweight flexibility. Soft pouches are the most compact but offer minimal impact protection. For checked luggage, always choose hard shell. For carry-on, soft pouches can work if your bag is not overpacked.

Ventilation is underrated. A sealed case traps moisture from a damp brush, creating conditions for mold and bacterial growth. The best travel cases include mesh panels, ventilation holes, or breathable materials that allow air circulation. If your case lacks ventilation, leave it slightly unzipped when possible to let moisture escape.

Size compatibility matters. Electric toothbrushes vary significantly in size. An Oral-B iO Series 9 is noticeably wider and longer than a Quip or Burst. Measure your toothbrush before buying a case, especially if purchasing a brand-specific case from a different brand. Universal cases solve this problem but sacrifice the snug fit of brand-specific options.

Extra storage is valuable. Cases with compartments for replacement brush heads, chargers, and adapters save you from packing those items separately. A case that holds everything in one spot means nothing gets forgotten in a hotel bathroom drawer.

Travel Cases by Brand

Oral-B Travel Cases

Oral-B offers travel cases specifically designed for their iO series and older Pro/Smart models. The iO case features a magnetic closure and custom interior that holds the magnetic charger alongside the handle. For older Oral-B models using inductive chargers, third-party cases are often the better option since the bulkier charger needs more space.

Many premium Oral-B kits include a travel case in the box. The iO Series 7, 8, 9, and 10 all ship with premium travel cases. If you own one of these models, check whether you already have the case before purchasing separately.

Sonicare Travel Cases

Philips Sonicare's charging travel case is genuinely useful. It charges the toothbrush via USB while the brush sits inside the case, which means you can charge overnight in a hotel room without any additional charger. This is a standout feature that no other brand currently matches in an integrated case.

The DiamondClean and ExpertClean series come with charging travel cases included. The ProtectiveClean and 4100 series do not include travel cases, but the same Sonicare charging case is compatible and sold separately.

Quip and Burst Cases

Quip's slim design has a built-in travel cover that snaps over the brush head. The Quip handle itself is designed as a travel case, so no separate case is needed. This is one of Quip's strongest selling points for frequent travelers.

Burst sells a slim hard case designed specifically for their sonic brush. It fits the handle, charger, and one replacement head. At $15, it is a solid option that matches Burst's overall aesthetic.

Travel Tip

Activate your electric toothbrush's travel lock feature before packing it. Most Oral-B and Sonicare models have a travel lock activated by holding the power button for 2 seconds. This prevents the brush from turning on accidentally in your luggage, saving battery and avoiding the vibrating suitcase surprise.

DIY Alternatives

In a pinch, a brush head cap and a ziplock bag work for short trips. Some travelers use hard eyeglass cases, which happen to fit most electric toothbrush handles perfectly. While these lack the dedicated compartments and ventilation of a proper travel case, they provide basic protection at zero additional cost.

Our Recommendation

If you use a Sonicare, the Philips Sonicare Charging Travel Case is the clear winner thanks to its built-in USB charging. For Oral-B iO users, the Oral-B iO Series Travel Case offers a perfect fit. For everyone else or mixed-brand households, the Nincha Universal Case is excellent value at just $10.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bring an electric toothbrush on a plane? +

Yes, electric toothbrushes are allowed in both carry-on and checked luggage. The TSA and international equivalents permit them without restrictions. The lithium-ion batteries in electric toothbrushes are well below the airline limit of 100 watt-hours.

Do I need a brand-specific travel case? +

Brand-specific cases offer the best fit and often include slots for chargers and extra heads. However, universal cases work well for most brands and cost less. Choose brand-specific if you want a perfect fit, universal if you want flexibility.

Should I let my toothbrush dry before putting it in a case? +

Ideally yes. A damp brush stored in a sealed case can develop mold and bacteria. Shake off excess water and let the brush air dry for a few minutes before closing the case. For short trips where you pack immediately, choose a ventilated case.

What size travel case do I need for an Oral-B iO? +

The Oral-B iO is larger than older Oral-B models. You need a case at least 9.5 inches long and 2.5 inches wide. The official Oral-B iO travel case fits perfectly, or choose a universal case labeled as compatible with Oral-B iO.
