Oral-B iO Series 9 vs 10: Is the Upgrade Worth $80?

Quick Answer No, the iO Series 10 is not worth the extra $80 for most people. The cleaning performance is identical to the Series 9. You are paying for the iOSense charging dock display and minor cosmetic refinements. Save the money and buy the Series 9 instead.

The Oral-B iO Series 10 launched as the most expensive electric toothbrush from a major brand at $379 MSRP. It sits just $80 above the already-premium Series 9 at $299. When two products from the same company share the same motor, the same brush heads, and the same app, you need to examine the differences carefully to decide whether the upgrade is justified. We did exactly that.

What the Series 10 Adds

The iOSense Charging Dock

The headline feature of the Series 10 is the iOSense magnetic charging dock. Unlike the Series 9's simple magnetic charger, the iOSense includes a small built-in LED display panel. This display shows real-time brushing guidance: a timer countdown, zone coverage feedback, pressure coaching, and a celebration animation when you complete a thorough session. After brushing, it displays your brushing score.

The iOSense is designed to eliminate the need to look at your phone during brushing. Instead of opening the Oral-B app, you simply glance at the dock sitting on your bathroom counter. This is a genuinely clever idea, but there is a problem: the Series 9's OLED display on the handle itself already shows your current mode, timer, and a smiley face for good brushing. Between the handle display and the app, the iOSense feels redundant rather than essential.

Refined Handle Design

The Series 10 handle has been subtly redesigned with a slightly more contoured grip and a refined matte finish. In hand, the difference is barely noticeable. Both handles are comfortable, well-balanced, and made from premium materials. The Series 10 adds a "Cosmic Black" color option that is not available for the Series 9.

That Is It

Seriously, that is the complete list of differences. The motor is the same. The brush heads are the same. The AI tracking is the same. The battery life is the same. The travel case is the same. The pressure sensor is the same. The cleaning modes are the same. Every specification that affects how well the brush cleans your teeth is identical between the Series 9 and Series 10.

Category-by-Category Breakdown

Cleaning Performance

Identical. Both models use the same linear magnetic drive motor, the same iO brush heads, and the same oscillating-rotating-pulsating cleaning action. Oral-B has not claimed any cleaning improvement for the Series 10. There is no reason to expect any difference in plaque removal, gum health, or whitening effectiveness between these two models.

Winner: Tie

Comfort and User Experience

Nearly identical. The Series 10's slightly refined handle contour is imperceptible to most users in blind testing. Both handles are comfortable, quiet (for oscillating brushes), and well-designed. The iOSense dock adds a small convenience by displaying brushing feedback without needing your phone, but this is a minor improvement over the handle's built-in OLED display.

Winner: Series 10 (marginal)

Smart Features

The iOSense dock gives the Series 10 a slight edge in smart features. Having a persistent display on your bathroom counter that shows your brushing score and coaching tips is convenient. However, the Series 9 delivers the same information through its handle display and the Oral-B app. The Series 10 is not smarter; it just presents the same information on an additional screen.

Winner: Series 10 (marginal)

Price and Value

The Series 9 wins value by a wide margin. At $80 less, it delivers identical cleaning performance with virtually the same features. The $80 premium for the Series 10 buys you the iOSense dock display, a slightly refined handle, and an additional color option. That is poor value by any measure. For $80 you could buy a year's worth of replacement brush heads instead.

Winner: Series 9

Our Verdict: Buy the Series 9

The Oral-B iO Series 9 is the clear winner in this comparison. It delivers the exact same cleaning performance as the Series 10 for $80 less. The iOSense charging dock is a nice-to-have gadget, not a must-have tool. Your teeth do not know which Series number you are using.

Choose the Series 9 if: You want the best Oral-B cleaning experience without paying for a redundant display dock. This is the right choice for 95% of buyers.

Choose the Series 10 if: You specifically want the iOSense dock display for phone-free brushing feedback, want the Cosmic Black color, or find the Series 10 on sale within $30 of the Series 9's price.
